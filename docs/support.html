ReefTracker Support

Last updated: 19 July 2026

ReefTracker is an offline reef-aquarium tracking app for Android and iOS. This page covers how to get help, report a problem, or ask a question.

User guide

New to the app, or wondering what a feature does? The illustrated ReefTracker User Guide walks through every screen — from your first aquarium to dosing calculators, ICP imports and backups.

Contact

For questions, bug reports, or feature requests, email bobrovsky@seznam.cz. Please include your device model, OS version, and app version (found in Settings → About), and steps to reproduce if you're reporting a bug.

Frequently asked questions

Where is my data stored? Do I need an account?

No account or sign-up is required. All your tanks, readings, and settings are stored locally on your device only.

How do I back up or move my data to a new device?

Use Settings → Backup to export a backup file (via the share sheet) and import it again on the same or a new device. On Android, the same section also offers optional Google Drive backup.

Is Google Drive backup available on iOS?

Not yet — Google Drive backup is currently Android-only. On iOS, use the manual export/import backup described above to move data between devices.

I imported an ICP report and the values look wrong. What formats are supported?

The microelements import supports common ICP lab report formats (including Fauna Marin and Triton exports). If a report imports incorrectly, email a copy of the report file along with which lab produced it, and we'll look into adding or fixing support for that format.

I'm not receiving reminder notifications.

Check that notifications are allowed for ReefTracker in your device's system settings, and that the specific reminder (testing, dosing, or maintenance) is enabled for the relevant tank. Some manufacturers' battery-optimization settings can also delay or suppress scheduled notifications — allowing ReefTracker to run unrestricted in the background usually fixes this.

How do I delete my data?

You can delete individual tanks, readings, or other entries directly in the app. Uninstalling the app removes all of its local data from your device. See the Privacy Policy for details.
